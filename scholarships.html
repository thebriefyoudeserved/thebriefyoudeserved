Scholarships & NGO Benefits

Veteran Service Organizations, National Programs & More

Scholarships and programs from veteran service organizations and nonprofits. These stack on top of federal and state benefits — apply to as many as you qualify for.

Deadlines, amounts, and eligibility change annually. Always verify with the organization's website before applying.

Stacking Strategy: The goal is to layer benefits so that free money covers your costs before you touch the GI Bill.
  • Layer 1: State programs (tuition waivers like Conroy, Hazlewood, etc.)
  • Layer 2: Federal benefits (Ch. 35 DEA stipend, TA, etc.)
  • Layer 3: Scholarships below (cover remaining housing, meals, books)
  • Layer 4: GI Bill (preserve as backstop or transfer to dependents)
Memberships Worth Joining:
  • American Legion (~$50/yr) — Opens access to additional programs and veteran advocacy. Legacy Scholarship does NOT require membership.
  • DAV ($300 life membership, or $10/mo) — Opens Auxiliary scholarships for dependents (up to $2,500/yr). Claims assistance is free regardless of membership.
  • ALA Junior (~$25-35/yr per child) — Start the 2-3 year clock for Auxiliary scholarships. Enroll younger kids NOW.
  • VFW (~$45/yr) — OEF/OIF qualifies. Opens essay contests and scholarships.
