VA Disability Claims Guide

Documentation, C&P Prep, and Common Mistakes

Everything you need to know about filing for the disability compensation you've earned. This isn't gaming the system — it's documenting the impact of your service.

Bottom Line Up Front:

Use a VSO (free). File during the BDD window (180-90 days before separation). Document everything while you're still serving. Don't minimize your symptoms.

Still serving? File BDD (Benefits Delivery at Discharge)
180-90 days before separation. C&P exams while active. Decision faster after separation. Back pay to day after separation.

Step 2: Document Everything While Still Serving

  • Go to sick call for EVERYTHING. If it hurts, get it on record.
  • Get all injuries and conditions documented in your medical record
  • Request copies of your medical records
  • Keep personal copies of deployment orders
  • Photograph any visible injuries or conditions

The best evidence is contemporaneous medical records from active duty.

Types of Service Connection

Direct Service Connection ▼

Requirements: (1) Current diagnosis, (2) In-service event/injury, (3) Medical nexus linking the two.

Example: Current tinnitus + worked on flight deck without hearing protection + "at least as likely as not" caused by service.

Secondary Service Connection ▼

Conditions caused or aggravated by already service-connected conditions.

Example: Left knee problems from compensating gait due to service-connected right knee injury. Or depression secondary to chronic pain.

Presumptive Conditions ▼

Certain conditions are presumed service-connected based on where/when you served.

Gulf War/GWOT: Chronic fatigue, fibromyalgia, IBS, undiagnosed illnesses

Agent Orange: Many cancers, Parkinson's, Type 2 diabetes, ischemic heart disease

Burn Pit/PACT Act: Respiratory conditions, certain cancers (expanding list)

C&P Exam Preparation

The Compensation & Pension exam is how the VA rates your conditions. This is critical to your rating.

DO

  • Review your claim file before the exam
  • Describe your worst days
  • Bring a list of all symptoms
  • Bring your medications list
  • Explain how conditions affect work and daily life
  • Mention all symptoms, including secondary effects
  • Be honest
  • Arrive early

DON'T

  • Go in unprepared
  • Only describe good days
  • Assume the doctor knows your history
  • Minimize or downplay symptoms
  • Be stoic or "tough it out"
  • Forget current treatments
  • Exaggerate
  • Miss the appointment

If you're having a good day: Tell them. "Today is a good day, but normally..."

Document Flare-Ups Specifically

  • How often they occur
  • How long they last
  • What triggers them
  • How they affect function
  • What you can't do during flares

Example: "My back pain flares 2-3 times per week, lasting 1-2 days. During flares, I cannot bend, lift more than 10 pounds, or sit for more than 20 minutes."

VA Disability Math (Combined Ratings Calculator)

VA uses "combined ratings" — not simple addition. Each new rating is applied to your remaining "able body."

Example: 50% knee + 30% back = 50% + (30% of remaining 50% = 15%) = 65%, rounded to 70%

Common Mistakes

1. Not Claiming Everything ▼
  • Don't only claim major issues — 10% ratings add up and can increase combined totals
  • Don't assume a condition is "too minor" to claim
  • Don't forget secondary conditions (conditions caused by your service-connected ones)
2. Insufficient Evidence ▼
IssueSolution
No current diagnosisGet civilian diagnosis before filing
No nexusRequest nexus letter from doctor
No STRsBuddy statements, alternative evidence
3. Poor C&P Exam Performance ▼
  • Downplaying: Describe your worst days honestly
  • Forgetting symptoms: Bring a written list
  • Appearing "too healthy": If it's a good day, say so
4. Giving Up After Denial ▼
OptionWhen to Use
Supplemental ClaimYou have new evidence
Higher Level ReviewDecision was wrong, no new evidence
Board AppealWant hearing or complex review

You have 1 year to appeal without losing your effective date.

After the Decision

If Approved

  • Note effective date (when pay starts)
  • Verify rating calculation
  • Enroll in VA healthcare
  • Apply for state veteran benefits
  • Update records for secondary claims later

If Denied

Don't give up. Appeal within 1 year to protect your effective date. Get a VSO if you don't have one.

Future Claims

You can always claim new conditions, request increases for worsening conditions, add secondary conditions, or reopen denied claims with new evidence.
